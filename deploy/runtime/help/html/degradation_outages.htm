Lifetime and Degradation

SAM's performance model calculates the system's hourly our subhourly electricity output for a single year. You can use the curtailment and availability factors and degradation rate described below to model system outages or a year-to-year decline in the system's output.

Note. SAM allows you to specify curtailment and availability factors greater than one, and negative degradation rates, so you can use the factors to adjust the system output upward or downward. You can also specify negative values for the curtailment and availability factors, although you should avoid doing so unless you have a reason to model negative electrical output.

By default, SAM's performance models run a simulation to calculate the hourly or subhourly performance of the system over a single year, and the financial models assume that the sum of the hourly values is the system's total annual output in Year one of the project life. The lifetime inputs allow you to define how SAM estimates losses in performance over time.

Degradation is an annual reduction in output due, for example, to aging of equipment over time. SAM applies the degradation rate to calculate the annual output in Years two and later: A degradation rate of 0 means that the system's annual output is constant over the project life, a degradation rate of 0.5% would mean that the system's annual output in each year decreases by 0.5% from the previous year's output. A degradation rate of -0.5% would mean that the system's output increases each year.

Note. For the geothermal power model, the performance model calculates the electricity generated by the system in each month over its lifetime rather than hourly over a single year. The degradation rate is not available for geothermal systems because the model calculates the system's electrical output from year to year.

Degradation Rates for the Detailed Photovoltaic Model

The detailed photovoltaic model allows you to choose whether the simulation runs over a single year or over the entire analysis period:

PV simulation for first year only

This is the default option. SAM runs a simulation over one year. The time step of the data in the weather file determines the simulation time step. For an hourly weather file, SAM calculates the power generated by the system for each of the 8,760 hours in one year. The total annual energy generated by the system in Year 1 is the sum of those 8,760 values. The total annual energy generated by the system in Years 2 and later is reduced by the degradation rate as described above.

PV simulation over entire analysis period

For this option, SAM runs a simulation over the entire analysis period defined on the Financial Parameters page. The time step of the data in the weather file determines the simulation time step. For an hourly weather file, SAM calculates the DC array power for each of the hours in the analysis period. For a 25-year analysis period, SAM calculates 8,760 hours × 25 years = 219,000 values. The total annual energy generated by the system in each year is the sum of the 8,760 hourly values for that year times the degradation rate that applies to that year.
Fixed and Annual Degradation Rates: Value and Schedule Modes

Fixed Degradation Rate

For most situations where you want to model an annual reduction in the system's output, you can use a single annual degradation rate that applies to all years of the project cash flow. SAM applies the percentage to the system's total annual electrical output value in years 2 and later.

For example, a degradation rate of 1% for a system with a net annual output of 100,000 kWh in Year one results in annual output values of 100,000 kWh in year 1, 99,000 kWh in year 2, 98,010 kWh in year 3, 97,029.9 kWh in year 4, etc.

Annual Degradation Rates

In some situations, you may want to assign different degradation rates to different years of the project cash flow. To do that, you use the annual schedule option to specify a table of degradation rate values. In Schedule mode, SAM applies the degradation rate for a given year to the annual output in Year one to calculate the annual output for that year.

For example, for a system with a Year 1 output of 10,000 kWh, if you specify a degradation rate of 0.5% for Year 5, SAM would calculate the annual output in Year 5 as follows: 10,000 kWh × (1 - 0.005) = 9,950 kWh.

To enter an annual schedule of degradation rates:

1.Click Sched to change the variable's mode from a single value to annual schedule mode. SAM displays the Edit button.

2.Click Edit to open the Edit Schedule window.

3.For Number of values, type the number of years for which you want to assign values. Typically, this number should be equal to or less than the Analysis Period from the Financial Parameters page.

Important Note. If you specify a number of values less than the analysis period, SAM sets the annual output for each year after the number of years you specify to zero, which may cause unexpected results. If you specify a number of values  greater than the analysis period, SAM ignores any values in the table for years after the end of the analysis period, which does not affect the results.

4.For each row in the table, type a degradation rate as a percentage of the system's total annual output in Year one.
5.Click OK to save the table and close the Edit Schedules window.
