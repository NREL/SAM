Initial Cost

The initial cost is the total installed cost from the System Costs page plus any additional costs and fees from the Financial Parameters page.

Note. The initial cash outlay (Year zero cost in the cash flow) is the Equity value in the Metrics table.

In general, the initial cost is total installed cost from the System Costs page less any cash investment-based and capacity-based incentives from the Incentives page and plus any additional costs and fees from the Financial Metrics page. The exact equation depends on the financial model as described below.

Initial Cost Less Cash Incentives

SAM reports the initial cost (described below for each financial model) and initial cost less any cash incentives from the Incentives page:

Initial cost less cash incentives = Initial cost - cash incentives

The cash incentives are the investment-based incentives (IBI) and capacity-based incentives (CBI). Cash incentives is the sum of all cash incentives:

Cash incentives = federal IBI amount
+ state IBI amount
+ utility IBI amount
+ other IBI amount
+ federal IBI percentage × total installed cost
+  state IBI percentage × total installed cost
+  utility IBI percentage × total installed cost
+  other IBI percentage × total installed cost
+  federal CBI rate × system nameplate capacity
+  state CBI rate × system nameplate capacity
+  utility CBI rate × system nameplate capacity
+  other CBI rate × system nameplate capacity

Initial Cost: Residential and Commercial

For the residential and commercial models, initial cost is the total installed cost from the System Costs page:

Initial cost = total installed cost

Initial Cost: PPA Models

For the PPA financial models, the initial cost is the sum of the total installed cost and total financing costs and development fees:

Initial cost = total installed cost + total costs and fees

Costs and Fees

Costs and fees are initial costs that you specify on the Financial Parameters page. SAM adds these costs to the total installed cost on the System Costs page to calculate the initial cost for the PPA financial models:

Single Owner

For the single owner model, costs and fees include debt costs, construction financing cost, and debt and equipment replacement reserves.

Total cost and fees = debt closing cost
+ debt up-front fee percentage × debt
+ other financing cost
+ debt service reserve
+ construction financing cost
+ major equipment replacement reserve

Leveraged Partnership Flip

For the leveraged (with debt) partnership flip model, costs and fees include debt costs, construction financing cost, debt and equipment replacement reserves, and development and equity costs.

Total cost and fees = debt closing cost
+ debt up-front fee percentage × debt
+ other financing cost
+ debt service reserve
+ construction financing cost
+ major equipment replacement reserve
+ development fee percentage × total installed cost
+ equity closing cost

All Equity Partnership Flip

For the all equity (without debt) leveraged partnership flip model, costs and fees include construction financing cost, debt and equipment replacement reserves, and development and equity costs.

Total cost and fees = other financing cost
+ construction financing cost
+ major equipment replacement reserve
+ development fee percentage × total installed cost
+ equity closing cost

Sale Leaseback

For the all sale leaseback model, costs and fees include construction financing cost, debt and equipment replacement reserves, development and equity costs, and the lease payment reserve.

Total cost and fees = other financing cost
+ construction financing cost
+ major equipment replacement reserve
+ development fee percentage × total installed cost
+ equity closing cost
+ lease payment reserve
